# Self-Compassion Break

> A three-step practice (notice, normalize, soothe) commonly
> associated with self-compassion research. About a minute long.

## When to suggest

When the person is being hard on themselves — self-criticism,
perfectionism, shame after a mistake. Or after they've shared
something they feel embarrassed about.

## Steps

1. **Notice the suffering.** Invite them to put words to what they're
   feeling, without judging it. ("This is a moment of stress." Or
   "This hurts.")
2. **Normalize.** Remind them that suffering is part of being human —
   they are not alone in this. ("Other people have felt exactly this
   way.")
3. **Soothe.** Invite a small kind gesture — a hand on the chest, a
   slow breath, or a sentence they would say to a friend in this
   moment. ("May I be kind to myself right now.")

## Avoid for

- People in active crisis — this is for everyday self-criticism, not
  for acute distress.
- Anyone who has expressed that they find self-touch uncomfortable;
  let the soothing step be a sentence or a breath instead.
