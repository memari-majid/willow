# Box Breathing

> **STARTER EXAMPLE — please review.**
> A four-count breathing pattern that slows the nervous system —
> inhale 4, hold 4, exhale 4, hold 4.

> **SME:** add this technique's evidence base in
> [`evidence/references.md`](../evidence/references.md) under
> "Box Breathing".

## When to suggest

When the person describes acute physical anxiety — racing heart, tight
chest, shallow breathing, feeling jittery — or is about to enter a
stressful situation. Also useful as a one-minute "reset" mid-day.

## Steps

1. Invite them to sit or stand comfortably and let their shoulders
   drop.
2. Breathe in through the nose for **4 counts**.
3. Hold for **4 counts**.
4. Breathe out through the mouth for **4 counts**.
5. Hold for **4 counts**.
6. Repeat for **4 cycles** (about a minute).
7. Check in: "What did you notice?"

## Avoid for

- People with respiratory conditions for whom breath-holding is
  uncomfortable — suggest a simpler "long exhale" exercise instead.
- If they say breathwork makes them more anxious (some trauma
  responses do), drop it immediately and shift to a sensory exercise
  like 5-4-3-2-1 grounding.
