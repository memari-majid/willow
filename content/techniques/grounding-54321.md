# 5-4-3-2-1 Grounding

> **STARTER EXAMPLE — please review.**
> A short sensory exercise that gently brings someone back into the
> present moment by noticing what they can see, touch, hear, smell,
> and taste.

## When to suggest

When the person sounds anxious, dissociated, ruminating, or "stuck in
their head" — and seems open to trying something practical. Especially
useful before a stressful event (presentation, conversation,
appointment) or in the middle of a wave of anxiety.

## Steps

1. Invite them to take one slow breath.
2. Ask them to name **5 things they can see** — out loud or silently.
3. **4 things they can touch** (the chair under them, fabric, the
   floor under their feet).
4. **3 things they can hear** — even faint sounds count.
5. **2 things they can smell**, or two scents they like if there's
   nothing in the room.
6. **1 thing they can taste**, or one flavor they enjoy.
7. Check in: "How does your body feel now, compared to a minute ago?"

## Avoid for

- Active panic attacks where the person is hyperventilating — try
  paced breathing first.
- Anyone who has just described a trauma flashback — let them lead and
  ask what they need rather than directing the exercise.
