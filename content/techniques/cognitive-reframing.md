# Cognitive Reframing

> A short reflective exercise that helps someone notice the thought
> behind a feeling and consider it from another angle.

## When to suggest

When the person has voiced a strong thought-belief that seems to be
fueling their distress ("I always mess this up", "Nobody actually
likes me", "I should have known better"). Best after they have felt
heard, **not** as an immediate response to a feeling.

## Steps

1. Ask gently: "What's the thought running underneath all of this?"
2. Reflect the thought back in their words.
3. Ask: "If a close friend told you the same thing about themselves,
   what would you want them to know?"
4. Invite a softer version of the thought. Not a fake-positive one —
   one that's both true and kinder.
5. Check in: "How does this version land for you?"

## Avoid for

- The middle of an acute emotional moment (someone crying, in panic,
  describing a fresh trauma). Reframing too early can feel
  invalidating.
- Anything that sounds like a fixed belief tied to an identity
  ("I'm a bad person") — that needs more space than a reframe.
